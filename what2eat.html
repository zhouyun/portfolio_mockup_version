What2eat

A mobile app helping people decide what to eat

Research, design

by Jay Wennington at unsplash

  • What to eat?

    You might be familiar with this scenario, you survive the whole day of work, come home and have no idea of what to eat for dinner. You find the food you always have is boring, but you don’t want to take the risk of trying new dishes. Or even worse, you don’t have a clue of what food you should take a risk on and try.

  • Choices are better than an open question.

    Based on my research, I focused on making the “What” question into a “Which” question. So I decided to build an app to simplify the process.

  • “Yes or No” vs “Which is better”

    It’s easier to have a preference than a standard. A preference involves a feelings which are quick to form, which standards involve reasons that take time to formulate. Machine learning can learn a user’s perference from photos of the dishes that are picked.

  • Choosing between is easier than
    from among?

    Then I focused on how many dishes to display for users to choose from. It truns out the ideal number to choose from is three or four. And choosing between the options was preferred by those who had difficulties with making decisions.

  • People love the design.

    “It will save me a lot of time of promoting restaurants to my girlfriend!” Said many users during testing. It seems that what to eat is a significant problem among couples.
