SmartPiggyBank

Physical toy helping children save money

Product design, prototype development | April 2015

  • How can technology help with B2B2C selling?

    For this 3-week project, our goal was to design a product to help Rakuten with it’s selling. After brainstorming, we decided to build a piggy bank backed by the cloud.

  • Start with a prototype

    We first built a prototype in an hour with kits from LittleBits. To count the coins accurately, we tested different ways for coins to fall into the piggy bank.

    Early prototype

  • It needs more effort and patience.

    The speed of the coins, the friction of the slide, and the spacing of the coin slots were determined to be the most critical issues. We iterated several times on the prototype to determine the best combination of factors to separate the coins.

    Shaun and I were adding the circuit to our fourth prototype.
    Final prototype

    We used an Arduino to calculate the savings. When the money reaches a certain amount, e.g. 1 dollar, it would send a signal to IFTTT, which will then send a notification of what kind of toys could be purchased.

    I’ve just finished the coding (and some cheesecake) from Ning, our video editor and cheerleader.
